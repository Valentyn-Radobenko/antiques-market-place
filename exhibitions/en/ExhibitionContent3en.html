History of VLP: Origins and Influence

The VLP group was founded in the early 1980s and has since become a symbol of French street art. Today, VLP members Michel Espagnol and Jean Gabaret continue to inspire new generations of artists. Their contribution to the development of urban art is difficult to overestimate: from the first aerosol drawings in the Galles district to prestigious exhibitions in the world's leading museums and galleries.
Exhibition "Before/After": A Mosaic of Time and Creativity
The exhibition "Before/After" is a unique opportunity to immerse yourself in VLP's 40-year career. Visitors will be able to see numerous works that demonstrate the evolution of the artists' style and techniques.

The exhibition includes:

  • First Spray Paintings: Discover VLP's first steps in the world of street art, where the city space becomes a canvas for creativity.
  • Iconic Symbols: Witness the birth and development of iconic images that have become recognizable symbols of urban art.
  • Thematic Transitions: Explore important themes running through VLP's work, from social commentary to aesthetic experiments.
  • Exceptional Works: Special exhibits gathered for this exhibition illustrate key moments in VLP's career, highlighting their influence and significance.

Respect for Craftsmanship and Heritage

Fluctuart pays tribute to VLP as key figures in French urban art. This exhibition not only showcases their best works, but also gives visitors the opportunity to understand more deeply the creativity and values ​​behind each piece. Discover how VLP transformed the streets of Paris and other cities into living galleries, where every stroke of aerosol tells its unique history.
